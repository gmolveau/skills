---
name: validate-idea
description: Interrogate a product or business idea to surface the riskiest assumption that could kill it, before any time is spent building. Use when the user wants to validate a project or startup idea, pressure-test whether an idea is worth pursuing, or find the fatal flaw in a concept.
---

Try to kill this idea. An idea that survives an honest attempt to kill it is one worth building; grill me until we know which it is. This is about the idea's merit — the problem, the customer, the demand — not how it would be built.

Ask the questions one at a time, and for each give your recommended answer.

Attack the riskiest assumption first. Every idea rests on a stack of assumptions, and it dies at the weakest one — so rank them by how fatal-if-false they are, and go after the deadliest before anything else. Walk the stack:

- **Problem** — is it real, painful, and frequent, or a nice-to-have nobody loses sleep over?
- **Customer** — who exactly, can we name and reach them, and are they already spending money or time to solve this today?
- **Alternative** — what do they use now, and why is this 10x better on the one axis they actually care about?
- **Demand** — what real signal proves they want it, versus polite encouragement?
- **Distribution** — how do the first 100 customers find it? (This is the assumption most often skipped and most often fatal.)
- **Economics** — does a sale make money once, and does that survive at scale?
- **Why you, why now** — what unfair advantage or shift in the world makes this winnable now?

Hold every claim to the Mom Test: "people said they'd buy" is worthless. Discount compliments, hypotheticals, and futures ("I would definitely..."); credit only real signal — money committed, time spent, a workaround they already built.

If a question can be answered by research — market size, existing competitors, pricing norms — research it instead of asking.

We are done when we agree on what must be true for the idea to work, which of those beliefs is the riskiest assumption, and the cheapest test that would kill it or prove it.
